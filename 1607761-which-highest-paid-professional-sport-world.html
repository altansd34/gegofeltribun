What is the highest-paid sport in the world right now? Top 15 list

There are numerous sports people play for money. Some are better paying than others. Usually, sportsmen get paid depending on the terms and conditions of their contracts, performance, sign-on bonuses, and even player popularity. The highest-paid sport in the world has millions, if not billions, of fans worldwide.

Professional athletes make significant sums from their salaries from playing various sports. They earn salaries, bonuses, and endorsement deals. Cycling is also called biking or bicycling. It is the use of a bicycle for sport, recreation, or transportation. Professional and amateur races are held mostly in continental Europe, the United States, and Asia. In 1903, the 21-day-long Tour de France was inaugurated. Cycling has continued every year since then, except during the world wars.

14. Pro-boxing - Average salary $51,370 pa

  • Official recognition: 688 BCE (23rd Olympiad)
  • Governing body: The International Boxing Association (IBA)
  • Average annual salary: $51,370 pa
  • Famous athletes: Muhammad Ali, Roberto Durán, Sugar Ray Robinson, George Foreman, Mike Tyson, Bernard Hopkins, and Wladimir Klitschko

Boxing involves attacking and defending with the fists. Boxers usually wear padded gloves and generally observe the code set forth in the Marquess of Queensberry rules. A pro-boxer wins a match either by outscoring the opponent or by rendering the opponent incapable of continuing the match.

Athletics, alias track-and-field events, are a variety of competitions in running, walking, jumping, and throwing events. Track-and-field athletics are the oldest forms of organized sport. Countries send teams of men and women to the quadrennial Olympic Games and to the official World Championships of track and field.

Field hockey is one of the most popular outdoor games. It is played by two opposing teams of 11 players each. They use sticks curved at the striking end to hit a small, hard ball into their opponent’s goal. Hockey began to be played in English schools in the late 19th century.

Tennis is played by millions in clubs and on public courts. In the game, two opposing players (singles) or pairs of players (doubles) use tautly strung rackets to hit a ball of specified size and weight. The ball bounces over a net on a rectangular tennis court.

NASCAR runs races primarily on ovals, including superspeedways, short tracks, and dirt tracks, but also road courses and street circuits. Since its inception, the NASCAR Cup Series has evolved into one of the most popular and best-paying sports in the world.
