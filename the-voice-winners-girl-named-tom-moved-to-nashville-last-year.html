The Voice Season 21 winners Girl Named Tom shared something that many fans likely missed over the past year — they moved to Nashville. Group member Bekah Liechty shared the news on Instagram Monday.

The Voice Winners Girl Named Tom Moved to Nashville

On Monday, Bekah posted an Instagram story to Girl Named Tom’s account in which she shared, “So, we moved to Nashville a year ago, and we originally thought this room would be a great guest room for when our mom came to visit, or whoever.”

Bekah then showed the room she was standing in, which was full of boxes, a guitar case, and a rack of clothes. She joked that there was “no room in the inn.” Later, she also shared a photo of the siblings’ mom at the house wearing a Girl Named Tom T-shirt, writing, “girl named MOM.”

The trio, who won The Voice on Team Kelly back in 2021, is originally from Pettisville, Ohio. If it comes as a surprise that they moved to Nashville last year, that’s probably because the group has spent so much time touring.

The Group Is Going on Tour This Year

Girl Named Tom spent much of last year performing for fans around the country before embarking on a holiday tour with the a cappella group Pentatonix. They also recently kicked off a new tour of their own.

The tour began earlier this month, with dates lined up into May. Guests on the tour include their fellow Season 21 contestant Bella DeNapoli, as well as Anna Vaus, Marielle Kraft, and Brandon Ratcliff.

The group has been busy with more than just touring since they won The Voice. They also released a Christmas EP last year and made their Grand Ole Opry debut in October. The siblings even hit No. 1 on iTunes after performing on The Voice Season 22 finale last December.
